Krill Fishing Threatens the Antarctic

03/22/2008

by: Juliette Jowit

Due to the high demand for health supplements such as Omega 3 oil and fish farm food, many have resorted to overfishing krill. Krill are small crustaceans that provide food for whole ecosystems.

"Whales, penguins, seals, albatrosses and petrels - all those creatures we think are absolute icons of Antarctica - depend on krill,"

—Richard Page

Scientists believe krill have declined by 80% from the 1970s to the 2000s. They believe that the main cause of the drastic decrease in krill is global warming, while a good amount of the rest of the decline results from over-fishing. Both causes of krill decline are, in large, due to humans.

How Oil Spills Affect Penguins

Cerca 2017

by: Act For Libraries

Oil spills affect both nesting and health of penguins. A great example of how oil pills affect penguins is the Nightingale Island Spill. When the oil reached the shoreline, it mixed with small sediments which created a thick sludge. The affected areas were inhospitable so many nests were abandoned and a whole generation of young lost. Furthermore, surviving penguins were coated with the toxic crude oil. It burnt their skin, affected their buoyancy–making it difficult to swim, and caused health conditions ranging from impaired liver to death.
